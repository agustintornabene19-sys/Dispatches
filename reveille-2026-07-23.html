From the Branch

The Advance of a Reinforced Rifle Company (1944 German pamphlet, translated)

"A rifle company should advance with two platoons in the first line so that, as soon as it makes contact with the enemy, it can deliver a lot of fire. If a rifle company leads with a single platoon, its front will be too narrow and, as a result, it runs a greater risk of blundering into an ambush. Infantry guns and anti-tank guns cannot be towed through woods that lack proper paths. Thus, mortars will serve as the chief heavy weapon of a rifle company fighting in dense forests... All hands will maintain absolute silence while advancing. Leaders will use signals to give orders. If, at any time, forward movement stops, subordinate leaders will immediately and, without waiting for orders, establish all-round security."

— Bruce Ivar Gudmundsson, The Tactical Notebook · Read › [more · less · never again]
