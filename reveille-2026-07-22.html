"We're not finished at all"

"The Houthis, an Iranian proxy in Yemen, are threatening to expand the war in Iran into a broader regional conflict. At least three American service members were killed in the last week in Jordan and Iraq. And the Pentagon is running out of cash. But President Donald Trump is charging full speed ahead.

Trump is being increasingly squeezed by a costly and unpopular war he once said would last for 'four or five weeks,' which is still escalating in its 144th day. But as White House allies and GOP lawmakers fret that the extended conflict could cost the party the midterms, the president reiterated today, 'We're not finished at all.' 'If we left tomorrow, … we had a big success,' Trump told reporters during an Oval Office meeting with Lebanese President Joseph Aoun. 'But we're not leaving tomorrow.' He brushed off concerns that Houthi rebels would block Saudi shipping in the Red Sea... saying simply that 'if something like that happens, we take care of it.'

'We took over Venezuela. We now have a great relationship with Venezuela. We went to war with Venezuela, and the war ended in one day,' he said. But Iran is not Venezuela.
