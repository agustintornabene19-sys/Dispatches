Starmer's Van

The bright canary yellow. The name, 'Bishop's Move,' which sounds like the name of an episode of Inspector Morse about the murder of a gay country vicar... Solutions to Short Problem 10
"The following post provides the author's solutions to one of the sixteen short problems published in the very first (1930–1931) issue of the Infantry School Mailing List."

"(1) (Fully-Trained Troops) The company commander moves his 1st Platoon around the quarry to the woods to take the counterattack in flank. He moves the support platoon (2nd) by the shortest route to Hill C to fire on the counterattack. He uses the 3rd Platoon for protection to the front, advancing it abreast of the woods."

"(2) (Green Troops) The left platoon (1st) and support (2nd) platoon are both moved by the shortest route to Hill C to fire on the counterattack... This maneuver is simpler and easier of execution than that made by veterans... A simpler movement would be better even if it promises less decisive results."

"(3) (Green Troops) The company commander resumes the attack toward B, being hidden from view of tanks. He thus moves out of the path of the tanks, which he has no weapons to combat, and he attempts to parry the very strong danger of a tank panic by giving his men something to do, by occupying their minds with the attack toward B. The tanks will have to be dealt with by some other agency."

"(4) (Fully Trained Troops) The company commander moves his company rapidly into the woods W, where it has some protection against tanks, with a view of allowing the tanks to pass, and firing on the infantry following. Such a maneuver by partly trained troops would tend to result in great confusion; likewise it would be difficult to keep them from opening fire on the tanks, thereby leading the tanks to attack the wood."
— Bruce Ivar Gudmundsson, The Tactical Notebook · Read › [more · less · never again]
