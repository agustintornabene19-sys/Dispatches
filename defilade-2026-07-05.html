From the Field
Initiative and Obedience [more · less · never again]
Bruce Ivar Gudmundsson, The Tactical Notebook · ~9 min
"Is the giving of orders an art? Many will doubt it. They feel that nothing could be easier than to command. Yet anyone who thinks that way ignores some stubborn truths. Only the man who has learned to obey is fit to give orders. An order is justified only when the situation makes it absolutely necessary... Every order places limits on the subordinate. He accepts those limits willingly when he sees the necessity for them. In such a case, obedience does not degrade him. Rather, it becomes the free gift of a free man. But he submits only grudgingly when the order springs merely from a fondness for being in charge or a desire to display one's own power. The desire to dominate leads straight toward tyranny and provokes disobedience."
